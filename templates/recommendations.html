Dietary Recommendations

Focus on Whole Foods:

  • Prioritize fruits, vegetables, whole grains (like oats, brown rice, quinoa), and legumes.
  • These foods are rich in fiber, which helps regulate blood sugar and hormone levels.

Lean Protein:

  • Include sources like tofu, lentils, beans, fish, or chicken.
  • Protein helps with satiety and stabilizes blood sugar.

Healthy Fats:

  • Incorporate avocados, nuts, seeds (chia, flax), and olive oil.
  • These fats are important for hormone production and overall health.

Limit Processed Foods:

  • Reduce intake of refined carbohydrates, sugary drinks, and highly processed snacks.
  • These can worsen insulin resistance and inflammation.

Exercise Recommendations

Regular Physical Activity:

  • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week.
  • Examples include brisk walking, cycling, swimming, or dancing.

Strength Training:

  • Incorporate strength training exercises 2–3 times per week.
  • Building muscle mass improves insulin sensitivity.

Mind-Body Exercises:

  • Yoga, Pilates, or meditation can help reduce stress.
  • Stress management is crucial for hormone balance.
