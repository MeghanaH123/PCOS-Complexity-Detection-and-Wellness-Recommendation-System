Based on your {{ diagnosis_type }} diagnosis: {{ pcos_status }}

{% if pcos_status == "Early Stage PCOS" %}

Dietary Recommendations (Early Stage)

Focus on Anti-inflammatory Foods:

  • Emphasize fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
  • Include healthy fats like olive oil, avocados, and nuts.
  • Lean proteins such as fish, chicken, and legumes.
  • Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and red meat.

Blood Sugar Regulation:

  • Choose low Glycemic Index (GI) carbohydrates to prevent blood sugar spikes.
  • Pair carbohydrates with protein and healthy fats to slow down glucose absorption.

Exercise Recommendations (Early Stage)

Moderate Aerobic Activity:

  • Aim for 30 minutes of brisk walking, cycling, or swimming most days of the week.
  • Focus on consistency to improve insulin sensitivity and manage weight.

Light Strength Training:

  • Incorporate bodyweight exercises or light weights 2-3 times per week.
  • Building muscle helps improve metabolism and hormone balance.

Stress Reduction:

  • Consider yoga, Pilates, or meditation to help manage stress, which can impact hormones.
{% elif pcos_status == "Advanced Stage PCOS" %}

Dietary Recommendations (Advanced Stage)

Strict Blood Sugar Control:

  • Very low Glycemic Index (GI) diet with minimal refined carbohydrates.
  • Prioritize non-starchy vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats at every meal.
  • Consider portion control and regular meal timings to avoid large blood sugar fluctuations.

Gut Health Support:

  • Include fermented foods (yogurt, kimchi) and prebiotics (garlic, onions, bananas) to support a healthy gut microbiome.
  • A healthy gut can influence hormone regulation.

Nutrient Density:

  • Ensure adequate intake of vitamins and minerals, possibly through supplements under medical guidance.
  • Focus on foods rich in magnesium, chromium, and B vitamins.

Exercise Recommendations (Advanced Stage)

Consistent Aerobic Exercise:

  • Engage in at least 45-60 minutes of moderate to high-intensity aerobic exercise most days.
  • This is crucial for weight management and improving insulin resistance.

Structured Strength Training:

  • Implement a more rigorous strength training program 3-4 times per week.
  • Increased muscle mass is highly effective in combating insulin resistance.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT):

  • Consider short bursts of intense exercise followed by brief recovery periods, if medically cleared.
  • HIIT can be very effective for improving insulin sensitivity and fat loss.

Professional Guidance:

  • Work with a certified nutritionist or exercise physiologist to create a personalized plan.
